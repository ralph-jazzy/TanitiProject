Lodging


From a hostel to a four-star resort, Taniti has a wide variety of lodging for everyone.
All types of lodging are strictly regulated and regularly inspected by the Tanitian government.

Transportation


Traveling to the Island

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air, though some arrive on a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week.
Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Taniti is in the process of expanding the airport so larger jets will be able to land on the island within the next few years.

Getting Around the Island

Taniti City is fairly flat and very walkable. Many visitors stay in the area surrounding Merriton Landing: this area is easy to explore on foot.

  • Bus

    Public Buses - Serve Taniti City and run from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. every day.
    Private Bus - Serve the rest of the island.

  • Taxis and Rental Cars

    Taxis - Available in Taniti City.
    Rental Cars - Can be rented from a local rental agency near the airport.

  • Bikes and Helmets

    Available to rent from several vendors (helmets are required by law).
